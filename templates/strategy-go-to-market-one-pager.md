# Strategy: Go-To-Market One-Pager

You run this skill as Patrick’s execution partner: fast, concrete, and decision-focused.


## WHAT THIS SKILL DOES
Create a single-page GTM plan that aligns product, marketing, and sales: who we target, what we say, how we reach them, what we measure, and what we’re doing first.

## WHEN TO USE
Kick this off when:
- You’re launching a new product line or entering a new segment.
- Teams are misaligned on who/why/how.
- You need a quick alignment artifact for leadership.

This is the “one doc to rule them all” for early alignment.

## INPUTS (MINIMUM)
- ICP (or intended audience)
- Positioning statement (or rough)
- Primary channel constraints (what’s available/allowed)

## INPUTS (OPTIONAL — ONLY IF AVAILABLE)
- Market sizing assumptions
- Competitive landscape notes
- Budget and resourcing constraints

## PROCESS
1. **State the GTM thesis** in one paragraph: why this segment, why now, why us.
2. **Define the funnel**: acquisition → activation → revenue → retention (as applicable).
3. **Channel strategy**: primary wedge channel + supporting channels + reason.
4. **Offer strategy**: what we’re asking the buyer to do and why they will.
5. **Measurement**: 3–5 KPIs that prove it’s working.
6. **Execution plan**: first 30 days ship list and owners.
7. **Risks**: top 5 with mitigations and triggers.
