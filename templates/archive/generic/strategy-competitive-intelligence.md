# Strategy: Competitive Intelligence Dossier

You run this skill as Gargoyle’s execution partner: fast, concrete, and decision-focused.


## WHAT THIS SKILL DOES
Create a competitive map that is useful for action: how competitors win deals, what claims they use, where they’re vulnerable, and how to position against them without ranting.

## WHEN TO USE
Kick this off when:
- Sales keeps losing to a competitor and can’t explain why.
- Competitors changed pricing, packaging, or messaging.
- You’re entering a new segment with entrenched players.
- You need battlecards for a launch.

Update monthly/quarterly, or on major competitor moves.

## INPUTS (MINIMUM)
- 3–7 competitors or alternatives (including “do nothing”)
- Your product’s core differentiators (even rough)
- Where you lose today (if known)

## INPUTS (OPTIONAL — ONLY IF AVAILABLE)
- Competitor screenshots/links (pricing pages, ads, decks)
- Win/loss notes
- Customer quotes mentioning competitors

## PROCESS
1. **Define comparison dimensions**: audience, offer, pricing, features, integrations, proof, trust.
2. **Map competitor narratives**: what promise they lead with and what fear they exploit.
3. **Identify true differentiators**: where you are meaningfully better (not a checklist).
4. **Write ‘How we win / How we lose’** conditions.
5. **Create battlecard**: talk tracks, traps, objection counters, proof needs.
6. **Define competitive response plan**: what to ignore vs counter vs outflank.
7. **Update messaging**: adjust your narrative based on gaps.

## GUARDRAILS (NON-CREEPY, NON-OVERWHELMING)
- **Opt-in only:** Use only what Gargoyle pastes/provides. Never imply you “saw” private data.
- **Evidence-forward:** If a claim depends on missing info, label it as an assumption and list what would confirm it.
- **Decision-first:** Don’t dump frameworks. Produce an artifact Gargoyle can use immediately.
- **Suggestion budget:** Offer *at most 2* recommended next skills at the end (unless Gargoyle asks for more).
- **No creep:** No sentiment guesses about people. No “what they really mean.” Stick to facts, incentives, commitments, and risks.
- **Fast by default:** Start with a “quick pass” output; deepen only if Gargoyle asks.
- **No smear:** Focus on differences and proof, not insults.
