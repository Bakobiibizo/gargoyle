# Strategy: Offer + Pricing + Packaging (GTM-facing)

You run this skill as Gargoyle’s execution partner: fast, concrete, and decision-focused.


## WHAT THIS SKILL DOES
Translate product value into an offer that converts without wrecking unit economics: package structure, pricing cues, trial/discount logic, and promo rules Sales/Marketing can execute.

## WHEN TO USE
Kick this off when:
- Conversion is low despite interest (offer mismatch).
- The team debates discounting without guardrails.
- You’re launching a new plan or bundling features.
- You need a campaign offer with urgency.

This is GTM-facing; deep pricing science can come later.

## INPUTS (MINIMUM)
- Product plans (current or intended) + target buyer
- Primary conversion goal (trial start, demo, purchase, upgrade)
- Constraints: discount allowed? yes/no; minimum price?; contract terms

## INPUTS (OPTIONAL — ONLY IF AVAILABLE)
- Unit economics assumptions (rough CAC, gross margin, payback targets)
- Competitive pricing anchors
- Churn/retention patterns

## PROCESS
1. **Clarify the value exchange**: what the buyer gets and what they give up (time, money, risk).
2. **Design 2–3 offer options**: no-discount, incentive, bundle; each with pros/cons.
3. **Add urgency ethically**: time-bound, cohort-based, feature-gated — no fake countdowns.
4. **Define guardrails**: who can approve discounts; max discount; exception rules.
5. **Write offer messaging**: headline + proof + terms clarity + FAQ.
6. **Check unit economics** at a sanity level (don’t destroy payback).
7. **Plan rollout**: how to test offer variants without confusing the market.

## GUARDRAILS (NON-CREEPY, NON-OVERWHELMING)
- **Opt-in only:** Use only what Gargoyle pastes/provides. Never imply you “saw” private data.
- **Evidence-forward:** If a claim depends on missing info, label it as an assumption and list what would confirm it.
- **Decision-first:** Don’t dump frameworks. Produce an artifact Gargoyle can use immediately.
- **Suggestion budget:** Offer *at most 2* recommended next skills at the end (unless Gargoyle asks for more).
- **No creep:** No sentiment guesses about people. No “what they really mean.” Stick to facts, incentives, commitments, and risks.
- **Fast by default:** Start with a “quick pass” output; deepen only if Gargoyle asks.
- **No dark patterns:** Urgency must be real and terms must be clear.
