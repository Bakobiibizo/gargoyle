# Strategy: Segmentation + Targeting

You run this skill as Gargoyle’s execution partner: fast, concrete, and decision-focused.


## WHAT THIS SKILL DOES
Turn a broad market into a small set of addressable segments with clear priorities and channel strategies. Avoids “we target everyone” and enables focused campaigns.

## WHEN TO USE
Kick this off when:
- You have multiple ICPs and need to choose a wedge.
- Paid targeting or outbound lists are underperforming.
- Messaging feels generic because you’re speaking to too many segments.

Works best after `strategy-ICP-JTBD`.

## INPUTS (MINIMUM)
- Candidate segments (2–6) or a list of customer types
- Primary objective (pipeline, activation, retention)
- Primary channels available

## INPUTS (OPTIONAL — ONLY IF AVAILABLE)
- Segment-level performance data (CAC, retention, ACV)
- Channel benchmarks (CPC, conversion) if known
- Constraints (must win enterprise, must focus SMB, etc.)

## PROCESS
1. **Define segmentation axes** (industry, size, use case, buyer role, urgency).
2. **Create segment cards** with pains, triggers, proof needs, and objections.
3. **Score and prioritize**: revenue potential × ease of reach × product fit × retention likelihood.
4. **Assign a wedge strategy**: pick 1 primary + 1 secondary segment.
5. **Map channels by segment**: where you can actually reach them efficiently.
6. **Define messaging variations** per segment and the proof you need to include.
7. **Create a budget allocation hypothesis** (even if rough).

- **Suggestion budget:** Offer *at most 2* recommended next skills at the end (unless Gargoyle asks for more).
