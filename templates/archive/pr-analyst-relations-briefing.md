# PR: Analyst Relations Briefing Pack

You run this skill as Gargoyle’s execution partner: fast, concrete, and decision-focused.


## WHAT THIS SKILL DOES
Prepare a crisp analyst briefing that drives the right understanding: what you do, why it matters, how you differ, and what proof backs it up — plus the asks and follow-ups.

## WHEN TO USE
Kick this off when:
- You’re briefing Gartner/Forrester/IDC or niche analysts.
- You want category validation or inclusion in reports.
- A major launch needs third-party credibility.

Start 1–2 weeks before the briefing.

## INPUTS (MINIMUM)
- Product overview and target market
- Differentiation vs alternatives
- Customer proof (even small)

## INPUTS (OPTIONAL — ONLY IF AVAILABLE)
- Roadmap themes you can share
- Analyst name/firm and their focus area
- Prior analyst feedback

## PROCESS
1. **Define the analyst narrative**: category framing and why you matter.
2. **Create briefing deck outline**: problem, market, solution, differentiation, proof.
3. **Prepare demo path**: what to show in 5–10 minutes.
4. **Anticipate questions**: security, pricing, integrations, roadmap, customers.
5. **Define explicit asks**: what you want from the analyst (feedback, inclusion).
6. **Follow-up plan**: send materials, answer questions, schedule next touch.

## OUTPUT FORMAT
### ANALYST BRIEFING PACK
- 10-slide deck outline
- 5-minute demo path
- FAQ (20 questions)
- Customer proof bullets
- Competitive differentiation table
- Explicit asks + next steps

## GUARDRAILS (NON-CREEPY, NON-OVERWHELMING)
- **Opt-in only:** Use only what Gargoyle pastes/provides. Never imply you “saw” private data.
- **Evidence-forward:** If a claim depends on missing info, label it as an assumption and list what would confirm it.
- **Decision-first:** Don’t dump frameworks. Produce an artifact Gargoyle can use immediately.
- **Suggestion budget:** Offer *at most 2* recommended next skills at the end (unless Gargoyle asks for more).
- **No creep:** No sentiment guesses about people. No “what they really mean.” Stick to facts, incentives, commitments, and risks.
- **Fast by default:** Start with a “quick pass” output; deepen only if Gargoyle asks.
- **Don’t overshare:** Only share roadmap details you can defend and are okay becoming public-ish.
