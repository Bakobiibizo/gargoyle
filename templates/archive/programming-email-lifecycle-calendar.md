# Programming: Email + Lifecycle Calendar

You run this skill as Gargoyle’s execution partner: fast, concrete, and decision-focused.


## WHAT THIS SKILL DOES
Create an email operating system: **what we send, to whom, when, and why**, with clear ownership and measurement. Prevents over-mailing and random blasts.

## WHEN TO USE
Kick this off when:
- Email sends are inconsistent or feel spammy.
- Multiple teams send emails and collide.
- You need lifecycle automation (onboarding, nurture, reactivation).
- You’re launching and need a sequenced email plan.

Maintain weekly during active periods.

## INPUTS (MINIMUM)
- Audience segments (customers vs trials vs leads) and email platform
- Max send frequency rules (per segment)
- Primary email objectives (activation, pipeline, retention)

## INPUTS (OPTIONAL — ONLY IF AVAILABLE)
- Existing sequences (copy/paste)
- Deliverability history (open rates, spam complaints)
- Key product moments (activation milestones)

## PROCESS
1. **Define segment rules**: who receives what (eligibility) and suppression rules.
2. **Set frequency caps** per segment and a collision resolution rule.
3. **Program the calendar**: newsletter, campaign sends, lifecycle triggers.
4. **Design lifecycle sequences**: onboarding (Day 0–14), nurture, reactivation, upsell.
5. **Write measurement spec**: open/click, activation, pipeline, churn, etc.
6. **Create a send checklist**: QA links, UTMs, rendering, personalization, compliance.
7. **Set governance**: one owner for the master email calendar; weekly review.
