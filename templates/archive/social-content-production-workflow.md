# Social: Content Production Workflow

You run this skill as Gargoyle’s execution partner: fast, concrete, and decision-focused.


## WHAT THIS SKILL DOES
Make social output consistent without chaos: define a pipeline from idea → draft → review → publish → engage → learn, with clear owners and SLAs.

## WHEN TO USE
Kick this off when:
- Social posting depends on one person’s bandwidth.
- Review cycles are slow or unclear.
- The team wants predictable output.

Best paired with channel playbooks and social calendar.

## INPUTS (MINIMUM)
- Who creates content (roles) and who approves
- Channels and cadence targets
- Tooling (scheduling tool, doc system)

## INPUTS (OPTIONAL — ONLY IF AVAILABLE)
- Brand/legal approval requirements
- Asset library location
- Community support expectations

## PROCESS
1. **Define the pipeline stages**: backlog → draft → review → scheduled → published → engaged → analyzed.
2. **Set SLAs**: review turnaround, revision limits, scheduling lead time.
3. **Create templates**: post template, hook library, proof block library.
4. **Define approval lanes**: low-risk posts can be auto-approved; high-risk require review.
5. **Scheduling rules**: avoid collisions with emails/launches; tag posts to campaigns.
6. **Engagement blocks**: daily comment/reply time; escalation path for sensitive threads.
7. **Weekly retro**: what formats worked and what to repeat.
